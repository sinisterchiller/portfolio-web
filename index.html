About Me

Hi! I'm a third-year Electrical Engineering student who enjoys working with embedded systems and building projects where hardware and software meet. Most of my time goes into designing circuits, experimenting with microcontrollers, and writing Embedded C to bring ideas to life in a clean, reliable way.

I've built projects using ATtiny2313A and ATmega328P, working with RGB LEDs, buzzers, pushbuttons, touch sensors, ultrasonic distance sensors, LCD displays, and timing-based features like debouncing and PWM. I also work with digital logic and VHDL on FPGA boards like the Zybo Z7, creating systems such as MUX/DEMUX circuits, access control logic, counters, and 7-segment display decoders.

Outside engineering, I'm usually gaming, exploring new tech, or building small side projects just for fun. I like making things that are practical, intuitive, and satisfying to use, and I try to bring a bit of creativity into every project I work on.
